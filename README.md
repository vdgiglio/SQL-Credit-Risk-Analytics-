This Credit Risk Assessment & Portfolio Analysis project represents my first end‑to‑end exploration of how raw financial data transforms into meaningful insights that drive lending decisions. Using SQL as the backbone of the workflow, I engineered a complete pipeline that extracts, cleans, and structures borrower attributes from Excel sources into production‑ready datasets. This process involved designing relational tables, validating data integrity, and applying analytical queries to uncover patterns in borrower behavior. Through segmentation, risk scoring logic, and exploratory analysis, I identified trends such as higher default likelihood among borrowers with elevated utilization ratios, inconsistent payment histories, and lower income‑to‑debt stability. I also evaluated how credit score bands correlate with delinquency rates and how portfolio exposure shifts when adjusting approval thresholds. These insights mirror the real decision‑making frameworks used by banks, fintech lenders, and credit risk teams, giving me hands‑on experience with the analytical foundations behind underwriting and portfolio management.
